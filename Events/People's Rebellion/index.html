    People's Rebellion

    For many years, the continental government of Celestia has been dysfunctional, if not outright impotent. The government was a two house, unelected parliament where both houses needed to agree in order for legislation to pass. The two houses, having entirely different desires for the country, squabbled and passed nothing.

    This was, for obvious reasons, upsetting to the people of Celestia. Civic works went uncompleted, taxes went "missing" or were misspent, and the cities and towns of Celestia largely had to make due on their own.

    Over time, the people's resentment simmered, then boiled, then exploded. A protest outside the tower particularly unhelpful member of The Circle became a burning-in-effigy of that member of the Circle. Enraged by the disrespect, the councilman opened his balcony, took one step out, and with a gesture and word, killed twenty five lawfully-acting Celestian citizens in a strike of lightning, before slamming the door shut again and returning to whatever arcane activity he had been working on.

    The Councilman's tower, it turns out, was not sufficiently defended from a violent mob. He did not survive the night, as what had been a protest became a riot. The revenge-seeking rioters were joined by a number of members of the Celestian Guard, several adventurers, and if some stories are to be believed, at least one Brightseeker of Imera.

    The remaining members of The Circle mobilized the local barracks of the Celestian Army to search for the rioters, without the approval of The Assembly. The illegal orders caused defections within the ranks of the army and further degradation of the popular opinion of the government in general, and the Circle in particular.

    From there, a loose organization began forming calling themselves the People's Rebellion. The "leaders", as much as such a loose group could have leaders, have called on the two houses to abdicate power and for a more equal system.

    Violence in the last several years has seen most of The Circle either fleeing Celestia entirely or being killed by the People's Rebellion after they showed a lack of contrition or willingness to cede power. The Assembly, for it's part, has been much more reasonable about their interactions with the PR, but even so, several small skirmishes and one prominent assassination of a high cleric of Pyros has kept the Assembly from claiming a bloodless transfer of power.

    As the Rebellion winds down, Celestia is in shambles. The People's Rebellion is set up for guerilla warfare, not governance, and they have been unable to form a national coalition that looks like it might lead to a true government. The churches, which people would usually turn to for guidance have either made a statement of noninvolvement with future governance, or are part of the ongoing problem. Occasionally, a particularly powerful member of The Circle will reappear and attempt to stage a coup or take over as a dictator, often starting by "removing" the currently most viable leader from the PR.

    It is likely things will get worse, before they get better.
