In Arcadia, the power and size of a god's divine portfolio is strongly related to the number of worshippers that the god has.  For this reason, the gods and their churches recruit aggressively in order to gain power.  

This has caused, in effect, a centralization of power into a set of five "Large" gods with large portfolios that have almost no overlap, and a much larger set of "Small" gods with smaller portfolios that eat away at the edges of the large gods' power. 

For example, Imera's domains include "Cities" as part of their dominion over civilzation.  All cities are, to some extent, under Imera's protection.  However, a specific city whose people believe in that city specifically as being "different" or unlike other cities for any reason may manifest a small god.  This does not remove that city from Imera's domain, but it does weaken their control, in favor of the city-gods control.

Another example:  Yrrasil's domain over life extends to the soul's cycle of life and death and possible rebirth, and for many, Yrrasil appears to the dying at the moment of death to act as a psychopomp, moving them to the next place.  For others, local rites and beliefs around death may diverge significantly enough from Yrrasil's practices that that a local death god may take that job.

With that all said, the largest and most powerful gods within arcadia do little to squash belief in other gods.  Whether they see that as below themselves, or see it as simply ineffective is a question they are silent on.

### The Major Gods of Arcadia
*  [[Artrix]]: The god of Knowledge, Magic, and Learning
* [[Etos]]: The god of Excess, Self-Expression, and Ambition
* [[Imera]]: The god of Civilization and Fire
* [[Pyros]]: The god of the Forge and Order
* [[Yrrasil]]: The god of Nature and Medicine
