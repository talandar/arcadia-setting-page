Pyrrhus is a mountainous, desert island with a large, active volcano at the center.  The mountainous terrain has significant metal and [[firestone]] deposits, and is prosperous as a result.  Pyrrhus suffered most after the [[100-hours war]], when many of the top politicians and bureaucrats were revealed to have been under control of the Abomination from the hollows.  The new king, [[Alexandre Sinter]],  is well liked and was spared any blowback from his father's corruption.  Most buildings in Pyrrhus are of mud brick or mortared stone, for insulation from the desert heat.

### Demography and Population

55% [[Human]], 20% [[Dwarf]], 5% [[Polder]] 5% [[Demon]], 5% [[Dragonkin]] 10% Other
The land of the red crystal is largely volcanic in origin, and very little plant life naturally grows there. The people of the red crystal are mostly miners, Firestone farmers, or merchants, who sell ores, firestone, and obsidian to other countries in exchange for food, wood, and other necessities.   Unfortunately, these two groups do not always get along. The miners accuse the merchants of not buying at a reasonable price, while the merchants accuse the miners of artificially limiting the supply to keep prices high.

### Government Structure

Pyrrhus has a hereditary monarchy, currently led by [[Alexandre Sinter]].  Barely twenty during the [[100-hours war]], Alexandre was pivotal in taking down his own father's corrupted regime, and was hailed as a hero by his people.  Still, a monarch is a monarch, and one does not retain the goodwill of a people for free.  Alexandre is known as a reasonably just leader, but there remain agitators angling for limiting the monarchy through a constitution or similar measure.
### Military

Previously one of the most powerful forces on Arcadia, the hundred-hours war was disastrous for the Pyrrhan armed forces. Entire battalions of soldiers were misled by corrupted captains, while others saw the deception for what it was, and refused to fight the war, sometimes suffering casualties from other Pyrrhan forces.

### Religion

[[Pyros]] and [[Imera]] are the most commonly worshiped gods in Pyrrhus.  However, Pyrrhus is home to one of the few gods with appreciable power outside of the major five: [[Melitae]], who holds domain over oases and water found when sorely needed. 


### Agriculture & Industry

Pyrrhus is known for it's exports of [[Firestone]], which naturally grows in clusters on the exposed volcanic rock here. Firestone farmers build intricate walls that stimulate the growth of firestone on their land. Additionally, mining is a large industry in Pyrrhus, as the magma from the volcano here is rich in many valuable ores, including iron, copper, gold, and rarer ores, such as adamantine and mithril. The people of Pyrrhus also export magically hardened obsidian, used as armor plating in sky ships.

### Education

Public education in Pyrrhus is a newer concept - introduced by Alexandre after the war.  It is an ongoing process, so in general, the people of Pyrrhus are not formally educated.  Many apprentice to a craftsman, or join the Stoneseeker's guild (the miner's guild) when they come of age

### Technological Level

Firestone acts as a tremendously dense energy source, allowing for a sort of steam-age technology that mixes with magic for dramatic effects.  Pyrrhus is not a technological superpower the way [[Brunhild]] is, but definitely holds it's own.  Pyrrhan-built [[Airship|Ariships]] are known for their speed and armor, but are notoriously uncomfortable to ride on at speed, and lack much in the way of cargo space.
